What Is a Tax Table?

By Triston Martin Updated on 2022-08-30 00:00:00
If you're unsure about how much to report on your taxes or how to calculate the right amount, a tax table may be helpful. Read on to learn about tax tables, including their use, how they work, and the IRS tax table for 2022.

The phrase "tax table" indicates a chart that depicts the amount of tax owed based on income received. The IRS offers tax tables to assist people in figuring out how much tax they owe &' how to calculate it when they file yearly tax returns. IRS offers four tax tables for single filers, married filing separately, jointly filing, and head of household.

According to tax filing status, the IRS provides various tax brackets in the tax table. Tax rates can be presented as a discrete amount, a percentage, or a combination of the two. Individuals, businesses, and estates all utilize tax tables to calculate their taxable income and capital gains. Tax Tables, also called "Tax brackets," are updated by IRS annually.

How does a tax table work?

Individual and business taxpayers pay an annual effective tax rate on their income.

Each entity paid tax based on the following factors, such as

  • Filing status (single, married filing separately, married filing jointly, or head of household)
  • Tax credits and deductions that apply
  • Exemptions
  • The amount of money earned within a specific tax year.

To calculate the amount of tax each taxpayer must pay can be calculated by taxation authorities and taxpayers using these factors and the annual tax rates.

Visit the IRS, Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) tax table, or other websites that provide a tax calculator that does the math for you, as long as you know your filing status and taxable income.

A tax table shows breakpoint income thresholds that different tax rates will apply above and below. But taxable income, not gross income, is the income used in tax tables.

For instance, the standard deduction for single taxpayers in 2021 is $12,500 ($12,950 in 2022).

A taxpayer earning $65,000 for the year and qualifiying for the standard deduction will have to pay income tax on $52,500 ($65,000 - $12,500) in 2021. Generally, the higher a taxpayer's taxable income, the more tax they have to pay.

The columns for each filing status in the tax tables are different, and the rows on the left represent distinct taxable income amounts. A tax table can trace an individual's tax due based on their tax filing status and the amount transferred to their income tax form.

How to use a tax table?

Once you know your taxable income and filing status, using the IRS's tax tables is simple.

First, spot the line that represents your annual taxable income. Once you've found that, turn to the right and enter the tax amount on line 16 of Form 1040 or Form 1040-SR using the number in the column corresponding to your filing status.

Various official IRS documents provide tax tables. The tax tables may spread across different pages in each set of documents.

The example below makes use of the tax tables 2021 1040 and 1040-SR Tax and Earned Income Credit Tables publication.

According to these tables, page 7 of this paper would be appropriate for a single person with a taxable income of $35,027. Then, they would find the line representing their taxable income, defined as an income of at least $35,000 but not more than $35,050. Then, they would turn their focus to the number in the single-filing status column to the right of this line. Form 1040 or Form 1040-SR inputs this number on line 16 as the tax amount.

Remember that this is not the final tax you owe. You will continue to make further changes for tax credits, federal income tax withheld, and further down on the tax return form.

It's crucial to calculate your taxable income and tax liabilities accurately. The IRS will recheck your calculations for mistakes. You may pay interest and penalties if you owe more taxes than you paid. If you determine that you overpaid for the year, you are entitled to a refund of the excess amount.

IRS tax table 2022

The IRS provided updated tax and earned credit tables, as mentioned above. It gives an income-based tax range and distributes the amount due according to your tax filing status.

The 2022 tax rates, however, were adjusted to account for inflation as they are every year. In the following table, there are seven federal income tax rates in 2022: 10%, 12%, 22%, 24%, 32%, 35%, and 37%.. Taxpayers having taxable income over $539,900 for single filers and over $647,850 for married couples filing joint will be subject to the maximum marginal income tax rate of 37%.

Bottom line

Individual and corporate taxpayers with moderate income levels frequently use tax tables. High-earners frequently combine itemized deductions with more complex tax rate schedules.

Tax tables, as mentioned above, differ from state to state and alter yearly. Investors must constantly ensure that they are using the appropriate tax tables based on their sources of income and place of residence.
