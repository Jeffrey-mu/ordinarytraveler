What Is the Stuttgart Stock Exchange (STU)?

By Triston Martin Updated on 2022-10-28 00:00:00
Learn how Stuttgart Stock Exchange (STU) is the second biggest securities market in the southern German state.

Börse Stuttgart, sometimes known as the Stuttgart Stock Exchange, as well. It is the second-largest marketplace of securities in Germany, and it is responsible for around forty percent of all trading involving stocks in the nation. This Stock Exchange was founded in the initial months of 1861, and currently, it is engaged in trading shares, bonds, fund managers, and involvement licenses.

Comprehending What Is the Stuttgart Stock Exchange (STU)?

In 1861, the STU was established and was temporarily shut down because World War I happened from 1914 through 1919, and it also halted for a while because of the Great Recession II. In 2004, it established itself as the first stock exchange in Germany to put a cap on fees. By 2008, all financial products were being traded using digital methods.

Nowadays, the STU is often regarded as the most important stock market venue for individual investors. Equity, collateralized contracts, securities, investment group units, and profit involvement licenses are all tradable assets available to individual investors. STU exchange takes great satisfaction in having the lowest possible pricing and the finest possible quality of execution.

In 2019, STU was officially listed as ninth among all other reputed European stock exchanges in market capitalization, with a total trading amount of 68.5b € across all types of investments. Based on the webpage of the STU, the marketplace provides high financial flexibility and implementation certainty, premium price efficiency, active cap tracking and intellectual order types, a truthful fee prototype, and trading subject to oversight under the legislation. These benefits can be found in the market's aggressive pricing value.

The exchange established the subsidiary BSDEX in September 2019, a properly regulated marketplace for exchanging bitcoin against the euro. The marketplace also has ambitions to provide transactions for Ethereum, bitcoin, and XRP against the €, as well as cryptocurrency assets. BSDEX is Germany's first financial product that adheres to regulatory standards for digital tokens.

STU is available to everyone who has a residence in Germany. Customers are required to be a minimum age of 18, to maintain this German account and to be nationals of a country that is a member of the EEA. Börse Stuttgart, widely regarded as an innovator in the blockchain industry in Germany, established a science-based steering committee for blockchain innovation and text categorization in 2019.

STU and Startbase

In the same month of 2019, this marketplace announced that it would be launching an internet start-up portal known as Startbase. The portal's primary objective is to make German fintech startups that are more accessible to the global startup community. Startbase provides a means through which the complete German startup environment can be searched for and located. It is the first virtual, English-speaking internet platform for entrepreneurs in Germany and is being presented as part of a relationship with the GSA in Berlin.

One of the stock market's primary objectives is to assist newly established businesses in their pursuit of financial backing, particularly at the phase of their development known as the IPO. Startbase presents an extra opportunity to put money into the development of Germany as a global leader in the entrepreneurial incubation industry. In addition, shareholders can build profiles on Startbase and connect with entrepreneurs through those profiles.
