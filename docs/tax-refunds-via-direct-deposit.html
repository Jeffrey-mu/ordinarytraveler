How to Get Your Tax Refund by Direct Deposit

By Triston Martin Updated on 2022-09-13 00:00:00
Direct deposit is easy to use. Once you've selected it as your return option in your tax application, all you need to do is enter the account number and routing number. or tell your tax preparer if you want direct deposit. You can use direct deposit even if you are one of the few people who still file on paper..

If you need the money, it might be uncomfortable and frustrating to waiting for your tax notice at the mailbox return to arrive in the mail at your address. Thankfully, the Internal Revenue Service can electronically direct deposit all or a portion of your refund of federal taxes. 80 percent of taxpayers choose this straightforward option.

Share the resources

You can ask the IRS to direct deposit your tax refund when you file your return. The account can be in your name, spouse's name, or joint name if you have it with your partner if you're married. You can choose up to three of them to split the money among numerous accounts.

How to Request a Direct Deposit

If you only want the money to go into one account, you can request a direct deposit on your 1040 tax return.

Using Form 8888, you can split your refund into three separate bank accounts. You can direct your return to your Individual Retirement Account (IRA) or utilize it to purchase U.S. Savings Bonds by submitting Form 8888. (IRA). The IRS processes the majority of returns in less than 21 days. Is that where My Refund is? The feature allows you to track the progress of your refund.

Verify your bank account information three times

Any errors you make when entering the information about your bank accounts on Form 8888 or your return are your responsibility. The agency cannot attempt to undo the payment and provide a second refund to the appropriate account. Paper reimbursements are covered by the Check Forgery Insurance Fund (CFIF), which pays non-receipt claims but not direct deposit refunds.

In the case of an Error

A missing digit that leaves the account or routing number one digit short is one error that the IRS might discover. In that case, Your return won't be accepted for validation, and a printed check will be sent to you immediately. The check will be mailed to the most recent address the IRS has on record for you. The same will occur if your bank rejects the deposit for whatever reason. Ask your spouse, partner, or a trustworthy friend to verify your knowledge if you want to be sure. You can typically call the IRS at 800-829-1040 from 7 a.m. to 7 p.m., Monday through Friday, to stop the direct deposit if an error is found before your return deposit is processed.

If the Wrong Bank Account Is Used to Collect Your Refund

If the routing number you provided on your return was inaccurate, get in touch with the bank where your refund was deposited. By searching the IRS website with your improperly supplied routing number, you can identify the bank.

Call the IRS to inform them that the deposit will be returned and request, if possible, that the bank's ACH manager send the refund check back to the IRS. Taxpayers in that circumstance might also want to consult with an attorney to evaluate their options.

The Taxpayer Statement Regarding Refund Form 3911 should be sent if the bank does not respond within two weeks.

If Your Tax Preparer Changes Your Bank Information

The Taxpayer Advocate Service, a component of the IRS, has received reports that some less-than-reliable tax preparers occasionally alter the direct deposit bank information to divert funds to their accounts. That is a fraud; therefore, if it ever occurs to you, see a lawyer to learn about your legal options.

How may the direct deposit information for the IRS be modified?

If you've already filed your return and need to amend your direct deposit information, call the IRS at 800-829-1040. If you wish to use an account other than the one you used in the previous tax year, you must provide the new information on your Form 1040.

Who should you contact at the IRS?

800-829-1040 is the primary IRS contact number for private persons. Businesses should call 800-829-4933, while non-profit organizations should phone 877-829-5500. For TTY/TTD support, dial 800-829-4059. 7 If you prefer in-person assistance, contact your nearby IRS office.

Create a direct deposit

In the return you are filing, include the nine-digit routing number of your bank. You can locate your bank's routing number on a cheque. Your bank should be able to provide you with your savings account routing number. Your mobile banking app should give you the information you need if you utilize an internet banking program.
